Roadmap to Cybersecurity

My current goal is to learn Cybersecurity and have a job as a pentester or an ethical hacker. Please note that this roadmap is not everything you need to become good. You need to dedicate yourself and put a lot of effort and hard work into this in order to become good at it. This 4-year-long roadmap can help you design your own roadmap (since it is different from a person to another) if you are also interested in this field:

  • Year 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity
  • Year 2: Intermediate Cybersecurity Concepts
  • Year 3: Specialization and Advanced Topics
  • Year 4: Real-World Application and Certification
  • Additional Resources

Year 1: Foundations of Cybersecurity

Goal: Understand the basics of Cybersecurity, networking, and operating systems.

  • Step 1: Learn the Basics of Computer Science
    • Resource(s): CS50's Introduction to Computer Science
    • Link(s): https://cs50.edx.org
    • Time Frame: 3 months (6-8 hours/week)
  • Step 2: Networking Fundamentals
    • Resource(s): Cisco Networking Academy - Introduction to Cybersecurity
    • Link(s): https://www.netacad.com/courses/cybersecurity/introduction-cybersecurity
    • Time Frame: 2 months (4-6 hours/week)
  • Step 3: Operating Systems
    • Resource(s): Linux Journey
    • Link(s): https://linuxjourney.com/
    • Time Frame: 2 months (4-6 hours/week)
  • Step 4: Cybersecurity Basics
    • Resource(s): Cybrary - Introduction to IT & Cybersecurity
    • Link(s): https://www.cybrary.it/course/introduction-to-it-and-cybersecurity/
    • Time Frame: 1 month (4-6 hours/week)

Year 2: Intermediate Cybersecurity Concepts

Goal: Dive deeper into Cybersecurity principles, tools, and practices.

  • Step 1: Security Fundamentals
    • Resource(s): Professor Messer's CompTIA Security+
    • Link(s): https://www.professormesser.com/security-plus/sy0-601/sy0-601-course-index/
    • Time Frame: 4 months (6-8 hours/week)
  • Step 2: Ethical Hacking
    • Resource(s): Cybrary - Ethical Hacking
    • Link(s): https://www.cybrary.it/course/ethical-hacking/
    • Time Frame: 2 months (4-6 hours/week)
  • Step 3: Hands-On Practice
    • Resource(s): TryHackMe
    • Link(s): https://tryhackme.com/
    • Time Frame: Ongoing (1-2 hours/week)

Year 3: Specialization and Advanced Topics

Goal: Choose a specialization within Cybersecurity and deepen your knowledge.

  • Step 1: Choose a Specialization
    • Network Security
    • Application Security
    • Incident Response
    • Penetration Testing
  • Step 2: Specialized Learning
    • Resource(s):
      • For Network Security: Cisco CyberOps Associate
      • For Penetration Testing: Offensive Security's PWK (Penetration Testing with Kali Linux)
    • Link(s):
      • https://www.netacad.com/courses/cybersecurity/cyberops-associate
      • https://www.offensive-security.com/pwk-oscp/
    • Time Frame: 6 months (6-8 hours/week)
  • Step 3: Capture the Flag (CTF) Competitions
    • Resource(s): CTFTime
    • Link(s): https://ctftime.org/
    • Time Frame: Ongoing (1-2 hours/week)

Year 4: Real-World Application and Certification

Goal: Gain practical experience and consider obtaining certifications.

  • Step 1: Internships or Volunteer Work
    • Action: Look for internships or volunteer opportunities in Cybersecurity to gain real-world experience.
    • Time Frame: Ongoing
  • Step 2: Prepare for Certifications
    • Resource(s):
      • CompTIA Security+
      • Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)
    • Link(s):
      • https://www.comptia.org/certifications/security
      • https://www.eccouncil.org/programs/certified-ethical-hacker-ceh/
    • Time Frame: 3 months (6-8 hours/week)
  • Step 3: Build a Portfolio
    • Action: Document your projects, CTF achievements, and any relevant work experience. Create a GitHub repository to showcase your skills.
    • Time Frame: Ongoing
